Blockchain Security Best Practices (Presentation)

Objective: Explore best practices for securing blockchain systems.

Instructions:

In this assignment, students will delve deeper into various aspects of blockchain security by researching and presenting on specific topics related to blockchain security best practices. As future blockchain architects, understanding and implementing these best practices is crucial to building secure and reliable blockchain systems.

Requirements:

Assignment Topics:

Assign each student or group of students a specific aspect of blockchain security. Topics can include secure key management, access control, network security, consensus algorithm security, or any other relevant security concern.
Research and Analysis:

Instruct students to conduct in-depth research on their assigned topic.
They should explore current best practices, potential risks, and common vulnerabilities related to their topic.
Presentation Preparation:

Task students with preparing a presentation that covers the following aspects:
Explanation of the security concern or aspect they are focusing on.
Discussion of best practices to mitigate associated risks.
Real-world examples or case studies to illustrate the importance of the topic.
Implementation guidelines for incorporating these best practices into blockchain systems.
Presentation Delivery:

Allow students to present their findings and best practices to the class. This can be done either in-person or through virtual presentations.
Discussion:

After each presentation, facilitate a discussion where students can ask questions and engage in meaningful dialogue about the presented security best practices.
Peer Review:

Encourage peer review, where students can provide constructive feedback on each other's presentations.
Assessment:

Evaluate students based on the following criteria:

Depth of Research: Did students conduct thorough research on their assigned blockchain security topic?
Clarity and Presentation Skills: Were the presentations clear, well-structured, and engaging?
Relevance of Examples: Did students effectively use real-world examples or case studies to illustrate their points?
Practicality of Best Practices: Are the proposed best practices practical and relevant for securing blockchain systems?
Engagement in Discussion: Did students actively engage in discussions and answer questions about their presentations?
Peer Review: Did students provide constructive feedback during the peer review process?
